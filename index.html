About the Lawsuit

Depo-Provera Has Been Linked to Brain Tumors

Recent studies have found a significant link between long-term use of Depo-Provera (medroxyprogesterone acetate) and the development of brain meningiomas — tumors that form in the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord.

The Medical Evidence

Research published in leading medical journals has shown that women who used Depo-Provera for one year or more face an elevated risk of developing intracranial meningiomas. The progestogen in Depo-Provera may stimulate the growth of meningioma cells.

The Legal Action

Lawsuits are being filed across the country on behalf of women who used Depo-Provera and were later diagnosed with brain meningioma. Plaintiffs allege the manufacturer failed to adequately warn about these serious risks.
